# LARP-Lite User Guide — a quick check of a short text

*[한국어](USAGE_lite.md) | English · For deep analysis, see the [Full-version guide](USAGE.en.md)*

Lite is the **one-screen condensation** of the full version — it quickly pulls out just the *hidden premise* and *missing evidence* from a short text (a tweet, a paragraph, a single claim). (Understand the full version and you understand Lite for free — Lite is a part of it.)

## Walkthrough

1. **Open a chatbot.** Start a new conversation at chatgpt.com or claude.ai (the free tier works).
2. **Paste the prompt.** Copy the *entire contents* of [`prompts/LARP_lite.en.md`](prompts/LARP_lite.en.md) and paste it as your first message.
3. **Paste the short text.** Send the text to check as your next message.
4. **Read, and you decide.** The tool pulls out the *one sentence the text silently leans on (the hidden premise)* and the *missing evidence*, shows them so you can ask "is that really so?", and stops. It renders no verdict — the decision is yours.

If the answer isn't satisfying, add **"more detail / a different angle"** in the same conversation.

## When to move to the full version

- The text is **long or complex** (a judgment, a paper; several entangled claims).
- You need to weigh **which hypothesis the evidence fits better** (e.g., substitute payment or not).
- You want to **check missing evidence and quote authenticity** too.

→ Go to the [Full-version guide](USAGE.en.md). There you read every output: the evidence ledger, the evidence × hypothesis matrix, and missing evidence.

## One caution

**Do not put real case records or referral materials into an external AI** (security). Practice on public texts (articles, forum posts, judgments).
